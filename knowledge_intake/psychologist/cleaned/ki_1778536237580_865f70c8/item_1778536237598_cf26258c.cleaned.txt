Therapeutic note about anxiety and self-regulation.

When a person meets anxiety, the first task is to notice body signals: chest tension, shallow breathing, and the urge to control everything immediately.

A useful practice starts with one question: what am I feeling now, and what is actually happening around me? This question brings attention back to the present moment and lowers the intensity of automatic thoughts.

If anxiety grows, it helps to slow breathing, name five objects nearby, feel support under the feet, and only then make decisions. This does not remove the problem, but it restores the ability to think more clearly.

In relationships, anxiety often hides behind control. A person may believe that checking everything will bring calm. Usually, control keeps the inner tension alive. A softer path is to speak about needs directly and look for real support.
